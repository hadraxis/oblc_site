# Olivebank LC Brand Direction

Status: internal reference

This brief defines the brand direction for the Olivebank LC website. It is a working guide for site design, homepage art direction, and future brand refinement.

---

## 1. Brand Context

Olivebank started with an older shipping and trade idea. That origin still matters, but the current business is not a maritime brand in the literal sense.

The current reality is a modern personal contractor storefront.

The brand should feel like:

- a commercial operator
- a trusted individual
- a modern delivery system
- a person who gets things moving

The old town-square salesman, merchant, and delivery logic still applies. The difference is that the town square is now digital.

Think:

- cyber square
- digital trade
- routing
- delivery
- trust
- motion
- systems

Olivebank should feel like a modern operator in the digital marketplace, not a generic agency, not a corporate consultancy, and not a SaaS product.

---

## 2. Core Brand Idea

The strongest concept is:

"Old-world commerce, modern execution."

Or more plainly:

"The modern salesman in the cyber square."

This means the site should communicate:

- reliability
- practicality
- movement
- clean systems
- personal accountability
- commercial usefulness

The brand is not trying to look futuristic for its own sake. It is trying to feel current, useful, and sharp while still carrying the weight of trade and delivery.

---

## 3. Logo Direction

The current logo is acceptable as a short-term mark.

It does not need to be treated as permanent identity.

Brand rules for now:

- keep using the current logo where it works
- do not overinvest in a redesign before the site exists
- treat logo refinement as a later, compartmentalized update
- do not let logo uncertainty block the site build

The logo can be improved later if needed, but the website brand system should not depend on that future work.

---

## 4. Visual Direction

The site should feel cool, memorable, modern, commercial, and personal.

It should not feel:

- corporate
- generic
- templated
- sterile
- like a default startup theme

Recommended visual themes:

- merchant-modern
- cyber square operator
- digital trade and delivery
- personal storefront with authority
- premium but grounded

The look should suggest a person with taste and competence, not a large firm.

---

## 5. Palette Direction

Green is allowed and should remain part of the system because the user likes green.

However, Olivebank should not be reduced to "the green company."

Palette intent:

- green as a supporting brand note
- deep neutrals for structure
- muted warm accents for richness
- strong contrast for readability and presence

Recommended palette families:

- deep green
- ink or charcoal
- parchment or bone
- brass or muted gold
- slate or stone

Color should support the brand story of trade, trust, and motion rather than just decorate the page.

---

## 6. Typography Direction

Typography should carry more of the brand than color does.

Recommended direction:

- expressive serif for headlines
- clean sans-serif for body text
- confident spacing and hierarchy
- editorial composition rather than UI-first sameness

The typography should feel:

- modern
- deliberate
- slightly classic
- high-trust
- easy to read

It should not feel like a default app or an overused consulting template.

---

## 7. Symbolic Vocabulary

The symbolic language should evolve from the old shipping/trade idea into digital movement and delivery.

Good symbolic cues:

- routes
- paths
- seals
- ledgers
- columns
- markers
- signal lines
- navigation
- exchange
- transit
- flow
- structure

Useful metaphors:

- merchant as operator
- delivery as workflow
- trade as service
- routing as automation
- trust as clarity

Avoid making everything literal. The brand should imply commerce and motion without turning the site into a shipping company parody.

---

## 8. Homepage Art Direction

The homepage should visually feel like a modern storefront with editorial confidence.

Recommended art direction:

- strong hero statement
- compact supporting copy
- sections with deliberate rhythm
- one or two signature visual moments
- asymmetric layout where useful
- subtle texture or atmospheric background treatment
- brand mark used with restraint and purpose

The homepage should make the visitor feel that this is a real operator who can be hired directly.

Art direction should support these ideas:

- the person is the product
- the LLC is the business surface
- the service is practical and outcome-driven
- the brand is personal but commercially serious

---

## 9. What To Avoid

Do not steer the brand into:

- generic corporate consulting
- fake agency language
- heavy SaaS aesthetics
- overly technical automation jargon
- cold enterprise minimalism
- motivational startup fluff
- meaningless modern design trends

Do not make the logo or palette carry the entire meaning of the brand.

Do not overbuild future offers into the current identity.

---

## 10. Scope Boundaries

This brief is only for the current Olivebank LC website brand direction.

Out of scope:

- CIAB
- future black-box offers
- productized future services
- separate landing pages for unreleased concepts
- full logo redesign work
- deeper identity system expansion beyond the current site

CIAB and other future offers are intentionally compartmentalized and should stay out of this brief.

---

## 11. Working Summary

Olivebank LC should read like a modern merchant-operator storefront for the digital age.

It should preserve the trade and delivery instinct from the original concept, but recast it as:

- digital delivery
- automation
- routing
- systems
- trust
- personal service

The site should feel cool, competent, and commercially sharp, with green as part of the palette and the current logo as a usable starting point, not a permanent constraint.
